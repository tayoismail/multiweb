Markup Calculator — Calculate Markup Percentage and Selling Price

Our Markup Calculator helps businesses and entrepreneurs determine the right selling price for their products. Whether you're setting prices for a new product line, evaluating supplier quotes, or analyzing competitor pricing, this tool makes it easy to calculate markup amounts, percentages, and gross margins.


Toggle between two modes: Find Selling Price (enter cost and desired markup to see the selling price) or Find Markup % (enter cost and selling price to see the markup percentage and margin). Understanding the difference between markup and margin is crucial for profitable pricing strategies.

The Markup Formulas

Selling Price = Cost × (1 + Markup%/100)
Example: A product costs $25 with 50% markup: Selling Price = $25 × 1.50 = $37.50

Markup% = (Selling Price - Cost) / Cost × 100
Gross Margin% = (Selling Price - Cost) / Selling Price × 100

How to Use the Markup Calculator

  1. Choose calculation modeSelect Find Selling Price to calculate the selling price, or Find Markup % to determine the markup on an existing price.
  2. Enter cost and priceInput the cost price of your product and either the desired markup percentage or the selling price.
  3. Click CalculateSee the selling price, markup amount, markup percentage, and gross margin instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Markup is calculated as a percentage of cost: (Price - Cost) / Cost. Margin is calculated as a percentage of selling price: (Price - Cost) / Price. A 50% markup on a $20 item ($30 price) is a 33.3% margin. They measure the same profit differently.

Markup varies by industry: retail typically uses 50-100%, food service 200-300%, software 70-90%, and professional services 100-300%. Consider your costs, competition, and target margin when setting markup. Always ensure your markup covers all costs and provides adequate profit.

Use the formula: Markup% = Margin% / (1 - Margin%). For example, a 40% margin = 40% / (1 - 0.40) = 40% / 0.60 = 66.67% markup. This conversion is essential when translating margin targets into markup-based pricing.
